subnetwork

A subnetwork is a part of a larger network that is focused on a specific function or task. It is a subset of the overall network that is designed to perform specific operations or provide specific services. In computer networking, a subnetwork is often used to organize a network into smaller, more manageable segments. This can help improve network performance by reducing the amount of traffic that needs to be transferred between different parts of the network. Subnetworks can also be used to segment a network based on functionality, such as using a subnetwork for authentication and authorization, and another subnetwork for file sharing and printing services. In addition, a subnetwork can also refer to a group of devices that are connected together within a small area, such as a building or campus. For example, in enterprise networks, different departments or buildings can be divided into separate subnetworks, which can help improve security and manageability by separating sensitive resources from the rest of the network. Overall, a subnetwork is a powerful tool for organizing and managing complex networks, and can help improve network performance, security and manageability.
