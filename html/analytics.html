Analytics is a systematic process of collecting, cleaning, analyzing, interpreting, and visualizing data to uncover patterns, trends, and insights. It involves understanding the structure and relationships within data and using statistical and computational methods to extract meaningful information. 1.2 Why is Analytics Important? Analytics is important because it helps businesses and organizations make informed decisions based on data and insights. By analyzing data about customers, markets, products, and operations, businesses can identify trends, preferences, and potential opportunities to optimize their operations and strategies. 1.3 How does Analytics work? Analytics typically involve several steps, including: * Defining the problem or question: Clearly stating the scope and objectives of the analysis. * Collecting data: Gathering data from various sources, such as surveys, databases, and sensors. * Preprocessing and清洗 data: Cleaning and transforming data to ensure its quality and accuracy. * Analyzing data: applying statistical and computational methods to extract meaningful information and identify patterns and trends. * Interpreting the results: explaining the insights and their implications in a way that's easy for non-experts to understand. * Visualizing the results: presenting the insights in a graphical or visual format, such as charts, graphs, and maps. 1.4 Types of Analytics There are several types of analytics, including: * Descriptive analytics: summarizes and describes the data, such as calculating central tendencies, dispersion, and distribution properties. * Predictive analytics: uses historical data to forecast future trends and outcomes, such as modeling sales or stock market trends. * Prescriptive analytics: provides insights and recommendations on how to achieve desired outcomes, such as suggesting the best course of action based on predicted outcomes. * Diagnostic analytics: seeks to understand the underlying reasons for observed phenomena, such as detecting patterns or anomalies in data. 2.
