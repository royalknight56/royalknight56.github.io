Practical Problems： In our daily lives, we encounter various practical problems that require us to think and act effectively. These problems can range from small, everyday issues to larger, more complex challenges. Whether it's dealing with a lost homework assignment or navigating a complex business deal, solving practical problems is an essential skill that everyone must develop. The ability to identify and address practical problems is particularly important in the professional world. In many fields, individuals are expected to have the skills to tackle real-world issues, often requiring creative and analytical thinking. For example, in engineering, one might need to design a new product or solve a problem related to manufacturing processes. In business, someone may need to optimize a supply chain, improve marketing strategies, or resolve legal disputes. Solving practical problems also involves practical skills and knowledge. For instance, in order to fix a car engine, one needs to know how the engine works and be able to use tools to diagnose and repair the problem.
