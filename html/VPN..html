**VPN: An Overview** With the increasing trend of online activities and networking, the need for a secure and encrypted medium for communication has become imperative. This has led to the advent of Virtual Private Networks (VPNs). In this article, we delve into the world of VPNs, their working principles, advantages, and disadvantages, and the various countries where VPNs are legal and which are banned. **What is a VPN?** A VPN is a Virtual Private Network, commonly known as a VPN. It is a technology that allows users to create a secure, encrypted connection to another network, such as the internet. The VPN mask's the IP address of the user, making it appear as if the traffic is coming from the network of the VPN service provider rather than the user's actual IP address. This helps to protect the privacy and security of the user when they are connected to untrusted networks, such as public Wi-Fi hotspots. **How does VPN work?** When a user connects to a VPN server, the VPN software on the user's device establishes a secure, encrypted connection to the VPN server. This connection is established using the Internet Protocol (IP) and Transport Layer Security (TLS) protocols. Once the connection is established, all the data that is transmitted between the user's device and the VPN server is encrypted using Advanced Encryption Standard (AES) 128-bit encryption. **Why use a VPN?** There are several reasons why people use VPNs. In countries with government surveillance, VPNs can help protect the user's data and communications from government oversight. **Which countries have VPN bans?** While VPN usage is generally legal in most countries, there are some countries where VPN usage is banned or restricted.
